Thorium

Thorium is a chemical element with the symbol Th and the atomic number 90. It is one of only two significantly radioactive elements that still occur naturally in large quantities. Thorium metal is silvery and tarnishes black when exposed to air. Thorium is weakly radioactive: all its known isotopes are unstable, with seven naturally occurring ones.

Occurence in India

India has reserves of thorium in sufficient quantity as compared to other parts of the world.The known reserves of thorium in India are estimated to be between 457,000 and 508,000 tonnes. Kerala, Jharkhand, Bihar, Tamil Nadu, and Rajasthan are the main producers.

Opportunities

Thorium is a great alternative to uranium because it is more abundant, cheaper, and more efficient. Because of the higher efficiency of thorium, the radioactive waste generated is minimal, which is in favor of the environment, considering the longevity and harmful effects of radioactive waste.

Challenges
